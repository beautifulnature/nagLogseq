- reflection
	- laws of reflection of light
		- the angle of incidence is equal to the angle of reflection, and
		- the incident ray, the normal to the mirror at the point of incidence and the reflected ray, all lie in the same plane
	- spherical mirrors
		- concave mirror
		- convex mirror
		- pole P: center of reflecting surface of a spherical mirror.
		  collapsed:: true
			- it lies on the surface of the mirror
		- centre of curvature of spherical mirror C
		  collapsed:: true
			- it lies outside its reflecting surface and is not a part of the mirror
			- C lies Infront of it for concave mirrors
			- C lies behind for convex mirrors
		- radius of curvature of the mirror R
		  collapsed:: true
			- is equal to PC distance
		- principal axis
		  collapsed:: true
			- line passing through the pole and centre of curvature of a spherical mirror
			- principal axis is normal to the mirror at its pole
		- Principal Focus `F`
		  collapsed:: true
			- point `F` where all light rays parallel to the principal axis falling on mirror surface reflected and intersecting at a point on the principal axis of the mirror.
			- focal length of the mirror `f`
				- distance between pole `P` and principal focus `F` is called focal length.
		- aperture
		  collapsed:: true
			- reflecting surface of a spherical mirror is by and large spherical. The surface, then, has a circular outline. The diameter of the reflecting surface of spherical mirror is called its aperture.
			- spherical mirrors of small apertures MN < R
				- R = 2f
				- means F lies midway between C and P
