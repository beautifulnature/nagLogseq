- VaR estimates the maximum potential loss an investment portfolio could face over a specific period at a given confidence level. VaR ranks past returns from worst to best, and then selects a specific percentile (e.g., 5th percentile) as the VaR, indicating that, historically, losses have exceeded this value only 5% of the time. VaR is used by traders to get a directional estimate of potential loss in value of their positions under normal market conditions.
